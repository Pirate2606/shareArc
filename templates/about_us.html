About ShareArc

ShareArc is the newest resource sharing platform on the market, tied to a fundamentally different business model. ShareArc is the result of a deep, open collaboration between a group of libraries, consortia, and companies, based on the notion that community infrastructure should be owned by communities, not by individual organizations. Its open-source code base is available for anyone to use, enhance, or support.

ShareArc was a direct response to a growing number of acquisitions in the library marketplace, leading to less choice, innovation, and influence. ShareArc seeks to drive innovation and agency through co-ownership of technology and through collaborative development. The initial functionality of ShareArc centers on consortial borrowing, but the community has a broad vision of supporting library collaboration and sharing through document delivery, digital lending, and more. ShareArc is based on an app-based software architecture to facilitate experimentation and innovation and to make it possible to adapt the software to individual library and consortial needs by selecting just the right set of functions. ShareArc has been designed by librarians for librarians in a user experience-centered approach, and the project is led by a steering committee with representatives from libraries, consortia, and developers.
